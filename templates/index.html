Frequently Asked Questions

The accuracy of facial recognition technology varies depending on a number of factors, including the quality of the images being used, the specific algorithms being employed, and the reference database being compared to. In general, however, modern facial recognition technology is highly accurate.

Facial recognition technology can only be used to identify individuals who are included in the reference database being compared to. If an individual is not in the reference database, the technology will not be able to identify them.

Face recognition systems use algorithms to analyze facial features such as the distance between the eyes, nose shape, and jawline. These features are then compared to a database of known faces for identification.

Face recognition is used in various applications, including security and surveillance, access control, authentication in smartphones, social media tagging, and law enforcement.
